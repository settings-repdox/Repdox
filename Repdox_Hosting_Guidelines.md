# Repdox Hosting Framework: Collaboration Guidelines

This document outlines the standard framework for external institutions and organizations hosting events in collaboration with Repdox.

---

### 1. Scope of Services
**Repdox handles:**
*   **Event Concept Design:** Specialized architecture for Hackathons, MUNs, and Workshops.
*   **End-to-End Execution:** Management of logistics, registrations, and scheduling.
*   **Marketing & Promotions:** Strategic outreach and participant lifecycle management.
*   **Participant Management:** Digital onboarding and support.

**Exclusions (unless specified):**
*   Legal liabilities of the host institution.
*   Guaranteed sponsorship funding (outreach is effort-based).
*   Academic content or internal curriculum development.

---

### 2. Roles & Responsibilities
*   **Client (Host):** Responsible for venue allocation, permissions, administrative approvals, and final decision authority.
*   **Repdox:** Responsible for planning, execution strategy, vendor coordination, and technical infrastructure.
*   **Shared Responsibilities:** On-ground volunteer management, branding alignment, and overall participant experience.

---

### 3. Timeline Commitments
To ensure high-quality execution, the following lead times are required:
*   **Hackathons:** 8–12 weeks.
*   **Model UN (MUNs):** 6–8 weeks.
*   **Workshops:** 2–4 weeks.

---

### 4. Budget & Financial Transparency
*   All costs and projected expenditures must be declared upfront.
*   **Fee Structure:** Fixed Fee, Revenue Share, or Hybrid model (to be defined in the specific MOU).
*   **Advance:** A **50% non-refundable advance** is required to initiate the project.
*   **Scope Drift:** Significant changes to the initial scope will require a cost revision.

---

### 5. Branding & Creative Control
*   All branding assets require mutual approval before public release.
*   Repdox reserves the right to use the event, its media assets, and outcomes in its professional portfolio.
*   Sponsor placements and visibility must be pre-approved by both parties.

---

### 6. Sponsorship Policy
*   Sponsorship acquisition is effort-based and not guaranteed.
*   All sponsors must align with the values of both the Client and Repdox.
*   Revenue split from third-party funding must be predefined.

---

### 7. Communication Protocol
*   A Single Point of Contact (SPOC) must be appointed by both sides.
*   Bi-weekly progress meetings (or as specified by the client and approved by Repdox).
*   All major approvals must be documented in writing.

---

### 8. Risk & Contingency Planning
*   **Redundancy Disclaimer:** Repdox does **not** maintain redundant backups for external speakers, institutional technical infrastructure, or secondary registration failovers.
*   **Force Majeure:** Coverage for pandemics, natural disasters, or unforeseen circumstances beyond either party's control.

---

### 9. Participant Experience Standards
Repdox guarantees a premium experience through:
*   Smooth digital registration and clear scheduling.
*   Fair judging mechanisms and professional moderation.

---

### 10. Legal & Compliance
*   Full compliance with local regulations and data privacy laws.
*   Strict enforcement of the Repdox Code of Conduct for all participants.

---

### 11. Cancellation & Refund Policy
*   **Non-Refundable:** The 50% advance and any already incurred vendor/logistical costs are non-refundable.
*   **Specifics:** Detailed cancellation deadlines and refund conditions will be defined on a per-event basis.

---

### 12. Post-Event Deliverables
Repdox will provide:
*   Comprehensive event reports and feedback analysis.
*   Media assets and archival data for the host's records.

---

### 13. Code of Collaboration
*   Strict adherence to agreed-upon deadlines.
*   No major structural changes within 15 days of the event.
*   Professional, direct, and transparent communication at all stages.
